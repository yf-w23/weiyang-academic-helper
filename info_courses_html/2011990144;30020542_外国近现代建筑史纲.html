课程内容简介：
自19世纪末期萌发的现代主义运动是世界建筑史上最为剧烈的变革之一。无论是建筑形态、建造方式还是理论论述，现代主义运动以及之后的建筑发展均与之前相对连续的建筑传统形成了强烈的反差。因此，“外国近现代建筑史纲”课程将讨论19世纪以来现代主义运动孕育、成熟、衰落以及此后外国建筑发展的历史。该课程不仅帮助学生理解近一百多年来激烈建筑变革的根源与过程，同时也为他们当下的设计训练提供思想养分。在综合吸收最新现代建筑史研究成果的基础之上，本课程将侧重讨论如下几方面内容：导致现代建筑出现的危机与条件；现代主义运动早期的实验性探索；代表性建筑师的思想与作品；现代城市规划理论的形成；现代主义盛期建筑与城市的面貌；对现代主义运动的批判与反思；以及20世纪末期以来新的理论与实践。教学目标是阐明历史发展脉络，厘清主要理论潮流实践倾向，并且对不同立场的优缺点进行批判衡量。 在获得基本历史素养的基础之上，建筑学本科生还将通过此课程了解主要的现代建筑与城市理论，学习相应的设计策略，初步确立对现代建筑发展的观点以及自身的建筑立场。
Course Description
The Modern Movement, which emerged in the late 19th century, brought about one of the most dramatic transformations in Western architectural history. The buildings produced during the Modern Movement and in the decades that followed differed fundamentally from earlier traditions—not only in form and construction methods, but also in theoretical discourse. In this course, these works are collectively referred to as “Modern Architecture.” We will examine the historical trajectory of the Modern Movement—from its origins and maturation to its eventual decline—and explore subsequent developments in global architecture. Students will be guided to understand the roots and dynamics of the radical architectural changes that have unfolded over the past two centuries. This knowledge will serve as essential intellectual grounding for their own design practice and critical thinking. Drawing on the latest scholarship in modern architectural history, the course focuses on several key themes: · The social, technological, and cultural crises that gave rise to modern architecture; · Early avant-garde experiments and alternative visions; · The ideas and built works of leading modern architects; · The formation and evolution of modern urban planning theory; · The aesthetics and logic of High Modernism in architecture and cities; · Major critiques of and reactions against modernist orthodoxy; · New theoretical and practical directions emerging since the late 20th century. · The overarching aim of the course is to clarify the logic of historical development, identify the core characteristics of major modern theories and practices, and critically assess their strengths and limitations. Beyond acquiring foundational historical knowledge, students will gain a nuanced understanding of key modern architectural theories and design strategies. On this basis, they will begin to articulate their own informed perspectives and develop a personal architectural stance.
