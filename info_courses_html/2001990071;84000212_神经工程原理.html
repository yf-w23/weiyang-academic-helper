课程简介：
对人脑信息编码机理及其解析与修复方法的研究是继人类基因组研究之后的又一科学前沿。近年来磁共振、多导脑电、微电极阵列、双光子显微镜、光神经遗传学等技术的发展，使得定量研究方法，特别是高通量信息处理方法在脑功能研究中发挥日益重要的作用。本课程介绍研究神经系统工作机理的主要工程方法，特别是对神经元放电序列、头皮多导脑电、功能磁共振等神经信号的分析方法进行系统的阐述。在此基础上，从工程角度总结了目前已知的神经系统的主要信息编码机理，并对神经解码的工程应用——脑机接口的研究前沿进行较为详尽的介绍。除课堂讲授外，本课程还将采用记录片观摩、实验体验、小组讨论等形式让学生了解神经工程学的最新技术与方法。
英文课程简介：
Understanding the neural code and repairing the impaired brain has been the next science frontier following human genome study. In recent years, new quantitative and high throughput technologies, such as MRI, multichannel EEG, microelectrode array, two-photon microscope, optogenetics, etc., has played important role in investigating brain functions. This course is designed to introduce major engineering method that can be used to study the neural functions, especially the analysis of neural spiking trains, multichannel scalp EEG and fMRI data. Based on this, the neural code and working principles of brain systems will be introduced from the perspective of engineering. This course takes brain computer interface as a comprehensive example of neural engineering, providing detailed and systematic introduction of this emerging technology. Besides lectures in the classroom, film review, experiment watching and group discussion will be employed to ensure the outcome of the course.
