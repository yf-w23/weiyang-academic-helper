课程简介：
高山区地质构造活跃、地形高差悬殊、水汽交换强烈、气候空间分异明显，是地质灾害的多发、易发区，如我国西部青藏高原地形急变带、欧洲的阿尔卑斯山区、北美西部落基山脉、南美安第斯山脉等地区均为灾害活跃区。其中尤以滑坡、泥石流灾害为主。滑坡，泥石流等作为一种常见的山地灾害，通常破坏沿途建筑物，并威胁铁路、公路和当地居民的生命财产，每年造成的大量的人员伤亡和经济损失。因此，大型工程的灾害评估的需求愈发迫切。了解滑坡，泥石流的启动，运动机理，可为地质灾害的准确预测预报和工程防治提供理论依据。在本课程中，学生将首先运用岩土力学和流体力学知识，了解滑坡，泥石流的基础，包括分类以及成因，固体物质组成，浆体流变特性，固体颗粒在流体中的运移。之后，学生将侧重了解泥石流运动机理，包括泥石流启动与产生条件，运动和堆积的理论模型，物理模型以及数值模型；最后，学生将学习地质灾害灾害防治，包括减灾防护手段以及工程设计，监测预警，危险性评估，以及风险管理。本课程综合了课堂讲授，分组讨论，室内实验，以及现场考察。为大型地质灾害风险评估以及防治工程设计提供基本知识，服务山区减灾和重大工程安全。
英文课程简介：
Large-scale geological hazards, such as landslides and debris flows, can cause significant financial loss. Landslides and debris flows can occur without warning and carries large amount of granular particles with wide size distribution (from large bolder to clay particles). Subsequently, it usually exerts powerful destructive loads on structures and threatens railway, highways, and the property of local citizens. In China, 29 provinces and 771 cities are threatened by debris flow. In recent 40 years, the number of fatalities directly caused by debris flow has reached more than 3700 every year. To effectively intercept this hazardous phenomenon, structure mitigation measures, such as barriers have been widely constructed along the predicted channel. In this course, student will firstly get familiar with basic concept of landslides and debris flow mechanism based on the soil mechanics and fluid mechanics knowledge. After this, student will learn the debris flow countermeasures design and practice, as well as the debris flow hazard assessment and risk management. The course is a combination of lecture, in-class discussion, laboratory test, and field trip. Students will not only strengthen the knowledge by experiencing the real laboratory flume test, but also get familiar with geological hazards counter measure design by site investigation.

Course content (32 hours / 2 credits)

1. Introduction to landslides and debris flows (2 hours)

2. Review of soil mechanics related to slope stability analysis (2 hours)

3. Initiation, transportation and deposition mechanisms (4 hours)

4. Granular mechanics (2 hours)

5. Rheological behavior (2 hours)

6. Dimensional analysis (2 hours)

7. Physical modelling (3 hours)

8. Debris flow countermeasures and barrier design (2 hours)

9. Geological hazards field monitoring and early warning system (2 hours)

10. Numerical modelling (6 hours)

11. Hazard assessment and risk analysis (2 hours)

Laboratory demonstration:

Flume test on debris-barrier interaction (1 hour)

Field visit:

Investigate the debris flow gully in mountain areas in Beijing and the corresponding countermeasures for debris flow mitigation (2 hours)
