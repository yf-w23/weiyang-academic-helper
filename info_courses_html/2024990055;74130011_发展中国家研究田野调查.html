课程简介：
本课程采用小班教学形式，围绕发展中国家研究中的田野调查这一核心方法展开。课程通过系统研读基于发展中国家长期田野调查的代表性研究成果，与同学们共同讨论田野调查在发展中国家研究中的认识论基础、方法路径与反思实践。本课程不将田野调查单纯理解为资料获取的技术手段，而是将其视为一种联结当下经验与历史过程、个体行动与制度运转、微观实践与宏观结构的研究方式。基于这一理解，课程不对田野调查的具体操作流程进行专题讲解。有关参与式观察、半结构式访谈、问卷设计、田野笔记等知识，课程鼓励学生根据自身研究需要，通过课后阅读与实践进行自主学习。 本课程的核心关切在于：对于发展中国家研究，田野调查为何是必要的？以及在不同研究路径中，田野调查能够实现何种研究目标？田野调查本身是高度情境化的，研究者往往需要在不确定性中不断调整研究策略、修正问题意识。因此，只有从认识论层面理解田野调查，才能回答“为何需要进入田野？”，以及“进入田野如何有助于提出针对研究问题的因果解释？”等基本问题；只有通过批判性阅读基于田野调查的经典与前沿研究，才能把握田野调查的多重路径；也只有持续保持对研究者反身性与自身位置性的警觉，田野调查才能在一次次重访中实现有意义的知识生产。
英文课程简介：
This course centers on fieldwork as a core method in the study of developing countries. Through systematic reading of representative works based on or related to long-term field research in developing countries, the course engages students in discussions on the epistemological foundations, methodological approaches, and reflective practices of fieldwork in this area of study. The course does not treat fieldwork simply as a technical tool for data collection. Instead, it understands fieldwork as a way of research that connects present experiences with historical processes, individual actions with institutional operations, and micro-level practices with macro-level structures. Based on this understanding, the course does not provide special lectures on the specific operational procedures of fieldwork. Topics such as participant observation, semi-structured interviews, survey design, and field notes are encouraged to be learned independently by students through additional reading and practice, according to their own research needs. The central concerns of this course are: Why is fieldwork necessary for research on developing countries? And what kinds of research goals can fieldwork achieve under different research approaches? Fieldwork is highly context-specific. Researchers often need to adjust their strategies and revise their research questions in situations of uncertainty. Therefore, only by understanding fieldwork at the epistemological level can we answer fundamental questions such as “Why do we need to enter the field?” and “How does entering the field help us develop causal explanations for our research questions?” Only through critical reading of classic and cutting-edge studies based on fieldwork can we grasp its multiple paths. And only by maintaining constant reflexivity and awareness of the researcher’s positionality can fieldwork produce meaningful knowledge through repeated engagement with the field.
