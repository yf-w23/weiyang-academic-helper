课程内容简介：
面对生物多样性危机、气候变化危机、公共健康危机等全球性挑战，构建“人与自然和谐共生”的未来已成为全球共识。本课程以“探索自然”为题，系统引导学生从不同尺度认识自然、体验自然、尊重自然，并重建与自然的连接。本课程是风景园林专业的入门课，也欢迎各专业同学选课，无先修要求。课程内容包括：（1）什么是风景园林？（2）大尺度自然——荒野自然与景观规划：国家公园与自然保护地、国家步道与世界公园、单日户外徒步指南；（3）中尺度自然——城市自然与景观设计（户外教学）：清华校园植物识别、基于植物的艺术创作；清华校园景观设计、自然景观漫游（Walking）与自然图析（Mapping）；（4）小尺度自然——园林自然与园艺操作（户外教学）：园艺基础知识、微花园营造。课程涉及丰富的户外教学，强调学生走出教室、走入自然，亲身体验身边的自然，感悟风景园林之美。
Course Description
In the face of global challenges such as the biodiversity crisis, climate crisis, and public health crisis, building a future in which “humans live in harmony with nature” has become a global consensus. Titled “Exploring Nature,” this course systematically guides students to understand, experience, and respect nature at multiple spatial scales, and to rebuild their connection with the natural environment. It serves as an introductory course in Landscape Architecture, while also welcoming students from all majors; no prerequisites are required. Course content includes: (1) What is Landscape Architecture? (2) Large-scale nature: wilderness and landscape planning: national parks and protected areas; national trail systems and emblematic parks around the world; and a practical guide to one-day outdoor hiking; (3) Meso-scale nature: urban nature and landscape design (field-based teaching): plant identification on the Tsinghua campus; plant-based art creation; campus landscape design; walking-based exploration (“Walking”) and analytical mapping (“Mapping”) of natural landscapes; (4) Small-scale nature: garden landscapes and horticultural practice (field-based teaching): basic horticultural knowledge and micro-garden making. The course features substantial outdoor teaching activities, encouraging students to step out of the classroom and into nature, to experience nearby environments first-hand and to appreciate the beauty of landscape architecture.
