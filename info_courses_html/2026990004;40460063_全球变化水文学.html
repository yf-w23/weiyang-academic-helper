课程内容简介：
全球变化水文学是地球系统科学专业的核心基础课程，与气候学、生态学、水文水资源、水力学等课程联系紧密。本课程聚焦全球变化水文学的基础知识与核心理论，着重培养学生定量分析地球水循环变化规律的能力，以及探究这些变化背后自然与人为驱动因素的方法。课程整合了多尺度水文观测与数值模拟领域的前沿科研成果及学术见解，涵盖原位观测网络、卫星遥感、陆面水文建模等方面的新技术与新方法。以水循环为核心纽带，课程系统阐释人类世复杂地球系统演变的驱动机制，使学生全面掌握：水圈变化的基本理论及分析计算方法；全球水系统观测、模拟与未来演变趋势预估方法；水循环变化对社会经济发展和人类福祉的影响机制及风险评估方法；以及应对全球水循环失衡的韧性提升理论与实践路径。
Course Description
Global Change Hydrology is a core foundational course in the Earth System Science program. It is closely connected with courses such as Climatology, Ecology, Hydrology and Water Resources, and Hydraulics. The course emphasizes developing students' ability to quantitatively analyze changes in the Earth's water cycle and explore the natural and anthropogenic driving factors behind these changes, focusing on the fundamental knowledge and core theories of global change hydrology. The course integrates cutting-edge scientific research and academic insights from the fields of multi-scale hydrological observation and numerical simulation. It covers new technologies and methods in areas such as in-situ observation networks, satellite remote sensing, and land surface hydrological modeling. Using the water cycle as a central theme, the course systematically explains the driving mechanisms behind the evolution of the complex Earth system in the Anthropocene. This enables students to master the fundamental theories and analytical calculation methods for changes in the Earth's hydrosphere, as well as the methods for observing, simulating, and projecting future trends in the global water system. Students will also learn about the impact mechanisms and risk assessment methods regarding how changes in the water cycle affect social and economic development, as well as human well-being. Additionally, students will learn the theories and practical approaches for enhancing resilience in response to imbalances in the global water cycle.
