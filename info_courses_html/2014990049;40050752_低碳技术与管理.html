课程内容简介：
面对近年来全球气候变暖、自然灾害频发、环境污染加剧、化石能源枯竭等国际重大问题，人类正面临由“高碳”模式向“低碳”模式转变的关键时期。低碳转型要依靠大力发展低碳技术与低碳产业，不断完善低碳发展的政策与管理体系。本课程正是为了满足未来低碳转型过程中对既懂技术、又通管理的综合性人才的需求而设置。面向不同专业、不同背景的本科生，本课程具备通识课性质，旨在掌握如何应对以气候变化为核心的全球性环境问题的基础知识，树立科学的低碳发展意识，为将来多行业、多领域中涉及碳中和与可持续发展相关的工作培养专门人才。 本课程将课堂讲授与课程设计相结合，鼓励自主学习与团队协作，强化实践与公众演讲能力；使用全英文课件（关键概念有中文标识），并全程英文授课；将不定期邀请低碳领域国际国内知名专家开展讲座，旨在开阔同学视野，了解国外低碳领域教学与研究模式，激发学习兴趣。 低碳技术与管理是快速发展的领域，知识与信息更新极快。因此，本课程鼓励同学以挑战性思维贯穿整个学习过程，课程讲授仅作为引导，希望同学能不拘泥于、并勇于挑战现有知识和观点，提出创新性的概念与想法。这也是本课程的初衷所在。
Course Description
The whole world is currently committed to adaptation against climate change, extreme disasters, environmental pollution and exhausting fossil energy by means of establishment of a low-carbon society. Such transmission is certainly necessitated not only in China but also worldwide. Development of low-carbon technologies management system will be the key approach. This course is aimed to train the undergraduate students with variable background for both technological and management knowledge. It is thus a cross-disciplinary course that encourages students to learn independently and collaboratively with the purpose to address complicated issues in energy, resource, environmental, economy and policy areas under the globalization circumstance. This course is not merely lecture and also includes quite a number of curriculum projects that require students to learn more after class and collaborate with team members. In course of the project design, students will be enhanced of abilities including but not limited to scientific writing, public speaking, literature hunting and communication skills. This course will be delivered in pure English environment. Furthermore, the students will be fortunate to stay with world famous experts in low-carbon fields and experience the cutting-edge research. Low-carbon technology and management is a fast developing field with frequently updated knowledge and information. This course extremely encourages students to challenge the conventional viewpoints and existing database of knowledge. The lecturer has the responsibility to lead students to think and behave in such creative and originative ways.
进度安排：

Module I. Introduction and Foundation of Low-Carbon Technology & Management (4 weeks)

 

W-1. Lecture 1. Carbon Emissions and Climate Change

1.1 Earth Energy Budget & Greenhouse Gases

1.2 Emission Contribution Factors (Kaya Equation)

1.3 IPCC Assessment Report and Climate Change Senarios

1.4 Radiative Forcing & Forcing Agents Analysis

 

W-2. Lecture 2. Global Climate Governance

2.1 UNFCCC – United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change

2.2 Kyoto Protocol and Market-based Mechanisms

2.3 Paris Agreement and NDC Mechanism (National Determined Contribution)

2.4 Updates to recent COP (Conference of Parties, UNFCCC)

 

W-3. Debate:

Role Playing based on information offered by NDC Tracker.

Roles include: China, USA, EU, Russia, etc.

(link to next lecture via discussing NDC measures raised by different countries)

 

W-4. Lecture 3. Overview of Technological & Management Approaches Towards Carbon Neutrality

3.1 Technological Roadmap for Global Net Zero Goal

① Efficiency Increase & Behavior Change;

② Electrification;

③ Renewable Energy (Wind&PV);

④ Green Fuels (H2 & liquid fuels);

⑤ Bio-Energy;

⑥ CCUS (Carbon Capture, Utilization & Sequestration)

3.2 Management Tools for Carbon Mitigation

① Carbon Accounting (LCA method);

② Carbon Pricing (Carbon Trading Market);

③ Techno-Economic Assessment for Emerging Technologies;

④ Green Financing for Commercialization of New Technologies;

 

Module II. Low-Carbon Technologies (6 weeks)

 

W-5. Lecture 4. Electrification & Renewable Energy

4.1 Traditional & Low-emission Power Facilities

4.2 Major Fields for Electrification

4.3 Wind Power

4.4 PV Power

 

W-6. Field Trip:

Wind & PV power plant around Beijing

 

W-7. Lecture 5. Green Fuels

5.1 Hydrogen Energy & Hydrogen Economy

5.2 Green Hydrogen-based Fuel: Ammonia

5.3 Green Carbon-based Fuels: Methanol for Marine Industry

5.4 Green Carbon-based Fuels: Sustainable Aviation Fuels (SAF)

 

W-8. Field Trip:

The Daxing International Hydrogen Energy Demonstration Zone

Fuel Cell Manufacture, H2 Vehicle, H2 Filling Station, etc.

 

W-9. Lecture 6. Bio-Energy & Waste-2-Energy

6.1 Bio-resource Energy (definition & classification)

6.2 Bio-gas from Biological Processes (inc. algal energy & bio methane)

6.3 Waste-to-energy facilities (focused on incineration)

6.4 Gasification for multiple fuels

 

W-10. Lecture 7. CCUS

7.1 CO2 Capture

7.2 CO2 Utilization

7.3 CO2 Sequestration

7.4 Deployment of CCUS worldwide

 

Module III. Low-Carbon Management (3 weeks)

 

W-11. Lecture 8. Carbon Accounting (LCA method)

8.1 Role & Standards for Carbon Accounting

8.2 Life Cycle Assessment Method

8.3 Step by Step Case Study

8.4 Tutorial

 

W-12. Lecture 9. Carbon Pricing (Carbon Trading Market)

9.1 Principles of Carbon Market

9.2 Design of Carbon Market

9.3 CCER Project (volunteer emission reduction)

9.4 Tutorial

 

W-13. Lecture 10. TEA (techno-economic Assessment) & Green Financing

9.1 What is TEA and key parameters

9.2 How to calculate capital costs and operational cost

9.3 Time value of money and discount rate

9.4 Financing a new low-carbon project

 

Module IV. Tutorial, exam & assignment (3 weeks)

 

W-14. Tutorial for assignment & exam

Design a new low-carbon project using the technological & management knowledge learned from this course. Work in team!

Review the major contents related to exam.

 

W-15. Exam

80 min for exam, and 15 min to explain the answers

 

W-16. Group Oral Presentation
