课程内容简介：
系统辨识是研究如何利用大量随机观测信号来建立复杂动态系统数学模型的理论和方法，是复杂动态系统分析、估计与预测、控制与优化决策、异常监测与故障诊断、智能维护等的重要基础，是控制学科的重要研究方向。系统辨识与机器学习有着密切的联系、共同的问题描述框架和基本原理，但也有不同的侧重点和理论体系。 本课程首先结合最小二乘法和正则化最小二乘法两种经典线性动态系统辨识方法，介绍其背后所蕴含的系统辨识基本概念和方法论，包括随机动态系统的基本概念和系统描述、辨识三要素的选择及优化问题定义、优化问题求解及模型参数和超参数辨识、辨识输入信号设计、辨识算法评价和统计特性分析、先验知识的利用等。在此基础上，遵循同样的辨识问题描述框架，课程将进一步介绍基于机器学习的非线性动态系统建模方法基本原理，包括基于普通神经网络的建模方法（如MLP、RNN、LSTM），以及几种专门针对非线性动态系统建模的机器学习方法，包括物理信息神经网络(Physics-Informed Neural Network-PINN）、非线性动态系统稀疏辨识（Sparse Identification of Nonlinear Dynamics- SINDy）和Koopman算子方法。在介绍这些机器学习方法时，将重点讨论其如何处理系统的动态特性，以及如何引入物理信息。此外，课程还将简单介绍基于时序大模型的时间序列预测方法。 在理论和方法介绍的基础上，课程将通过系统、综合的仿真实验、物理设备实验和复杂实际系统的辨识问题实验，使学生：(1)实现和验证课堂所讲授的辨识方法；(2)研究和验证各种方法的统计特性并理解其物理意义; (3)了解和探究系统的动态特性给统计建模和机器学习所带来的特殊问题和挑战，以及在机器学习中引入物理信息的重要性; (4)提高利用系统辨识的原理和方法对复杂动态系统进行基于数据的建模与分析的能力；(5)提高科研中常用的设计实验、并利用实验方法来验证理论结果和发现新问题的能力。
Course Description
System identification is the theory and methodology for building mathematical models of complex dynamical systems based on stochastic observation data. It serves as an important foundation for the analysis, parameter estimation and prediction, control and optimization, fault diagnosis, as well as intelligent maintenance of complex dynamical systems. As a key research direction in the field of control theory, system identification is closely related to machine learning. While they share common problem frameworks and basic principles, the two fields posses different focus points and theoretical systems. By introducing two typical linear dynamical system identification methods, i.e. Least Square, and Regularized Least Square methods, this course aims to introduce the basic concepts and methodology for system identification behind the specific methods. Based on this framework, the course will extend to the fundamental principles of machine learning-based nonlinear dynamical system modeling methods. This includes modeling methods based on common neural networks (such as MLP, RNN, LSTM), as well as several machine learning methods specifically designed for nonlinear dynamical system modeling, including the Physics-Informed Neural Network, Sparse Identification of Nonlinear Dynamics, and the Koopman operator method. A key focus will be on how these methods handle system dynamics and incorporate physical information. Additionally, the course will briefly introduce time series prediction based on temporal large-scale models. Experiments based on simulation, experimental setup and data from complex dynamical systems will be provided for students to help them implement and verify the system identification methods introduced in class, and enhance the capability to employ the principles of system identification for statistical modeling and analysis of complex dynamical systems.
