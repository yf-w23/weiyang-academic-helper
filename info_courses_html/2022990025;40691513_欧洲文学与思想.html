课程内容简介：
本课程通过介绍欧陆文学的代表性文本、主要人物、重要思潮和理论，指导学生批判性地掌握欧陆文学和思想史的谱系，既要对“欧洲中心主义”去魅，也要意识到欧洲文化遗产对全球各国文学和思想的重要影响。本课程分为六个板块：“文艺复兴和新古典主义”板块探讨古希腊罗马文化对人文主义者自我意识觉醒的意义以及揭露路易十四专制主义下贵族道德虚伪性的作用，包括彼特拉克、帕斯卡尔和拉罗什福科等；“从启蒙运动到法国大革命”板块探讨启蒙运动从对理性的极力推崇到犬儒主义式怀疑的转变，包括狄德罗、康德和福柯等；“浪漫主义与黑色浪漫主义“板块探讨浪漫派艺术主题，包括风景画与现代主体意识的建立和德国浪漫派领军人物施莱格尔兄弟的箴言体把世界浪漫化的尝试以及黑色浪漫主义对启蒙运动用理性照亮一切的反驳，也包括美国浪漫主义作家爱伦坡的怪诞小说和德古拉的故事；“现代主义”板块通过具体解读波德莱尔的诗歌、本雅明对波德莱尔的阐释、阿多诺对现代道德观的剖析来细致分析现代性这一宏大叙事；“存在主义”板块通过分析卡夫卡、萨特和加缪的作品以及介入文学的概念来理解存在的荒谬性以及如何给这些荒谬性赋予意义；“后殖民主义”板块通过赏析阿尔及尔战争这一半虚构半纪录片的电影以及法农关于阿尔及利亚独立的经典著作来探讨殖民主义的劣根性、解殖斗争中可能出现的误入歧途以及胜利果实被攫取的可能性。本课程的授课和文本阅读以中文为主，对于经典文本的重点段落，将选取中外文对照文本，让学生通过文本批评实践理解阅读原文的重要性。此课程不开放给外文系.
Course Description
This course aims to equip students with a critical understanding of the genealogy of European literature and intellectual history by exploring canonical texts, key figures, significant trends, and theoretical frameworks. It seeks to deconstruct "Eurocentrism" while emphasizing the profound impact of European cultural heritage on global literature and thought. The course is structured into six sections: 1. "Renaissance and Neoclassicism": This section examines the influence of ancient Greek and Roman culture on the self-awareness of humanists and the role of these ideas in revealing the moral hypocrisy of the aristocracy under Louis XIV’s absolutism. 2. "From the Enlightenment to the French Revolution": This section investigates the transition from the Enlightenment’s strong endorsement of reason to a more cynical skepticism towards it. 3. "Romanticism and Dark Romanticism": This section explores key themes in Romantic art, including landscape painting and the emergence of modern subjectivity. It also addresses the Romantics' use of aphorisms to romanticize the world and Dark Romanticism's critique of the Enlightenment’s rationalism. 4. "Modernism": This section offers a detailed analysis of modernity through Baudelaire’s poetry, Benjamin’s interpretation of Baudelaire, and Adorno’s critique of modern moral values. 5. "Existentialism": This section delves into the works of Kafka, Sartre, and Camus to understand the concept of existential absurdity and the ways to ascribe meaning to it. 6. "Postcolonialism": This section explores the inherent flaws of colonialism, potential pitfalls in the decolonization process, and the risk of the colonizers appropriating the outcomes of the colonized’s struggles. This analysis is supported by the semi-fictional, semi-documentary film "The Battle of Algiers" and Fanon’s seminal work on Algerian independence.
