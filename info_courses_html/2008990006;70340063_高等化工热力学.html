课程简介：
本课程包括三部分内容：高等经典热力学、统计热力学基础和应用热力学。在经典热力学部分，我们将从热力学四个基本假设出发推导经典热力学的规律与结论，即所有的热力学基本定律均构筑在基于日常经验的少量基本假设之上。这些热力学基本定律将进一步帮助解决与体系平衡性质相关的实际问题（包括现代化学工程、材料工程和生物工程之中的科学与工程问题）。在统计热力学基础部分，我们将介绍统计热力学的基础知识，并将之与宏观的热力学体系相联系。我希望化工热力学成为实用且强大的解决科学研究和生产实践的工具。因此我将参与课程同学分成若干小组，每个小组选择一个当前的热点问题。我将分组指导研究生同学采用一些热力学计算工具，例如分子动力学模拟、Monte Carlo模拟和DFT理论，来尝试解决当前某些热点科学问题。通过这门课，我不仅仅希望学生能够掌握热力学基础理论知识，而且具备能够应用这些知识和工具去创造性解决实际问题的能力。 课程主要内容如下： 1. 基本假设； 2. 热力学第一定律和第二定律； 3. 勒让德变换和热力学基本方程； 4. 平衡与稳定； 5. 纯物质和混合物性质； 6. 相平衡和化学反应平衡； 7. 熵的微观解释与波尔兹曼公式； 8. 系综及其对应的热力学势； 9. 涨落； 10. 理想（无相互作用）系统； 11. 分布函数与非理想系统； 12. 电解质溶液； 13. 热点问题讨论；
英文课程简介：
This course includes three parts: advanced classical thermodynamics (self-study with middle examination), essential statistical thermodynamics (important part with final examination) and applied thermodynamics (useful part with course report). Advanced classical thermodynamics will be presented as a deductive discipline based on four basic postulates. The laws of thermodynamics will be derived from a small number of postulates based on everyday experience. After those, the consequence of these laws will help us to address practical problems related to the equilibrium properties of systems, which appear in modern chemical/biochemical engineering. The second part of this course will give basic knowledge of statistical thermodynamics, which connects microscopic world with macroscopic one. We wish thermodynamics is useful and powerful in solving real problem in both science and engineering. Thus, students in my course will be divided into several groups, which focus different “hot” problems in research frontier. After this course, I wish students can understand basic theory of modern thermodynamics and apply thermodynamic tools to solve real problems. The list of topics to be covered includes: 1. Postulates; 2. First law & Second law; 3. Legendre transforms & Fundamental equations; 4. Equilibrium & Stability; 5. Properties of pure substance & mixtures; 6. Phase & Reaction equilibrium; 7. Microscopic interpretation of entropy & Boltzmann’s formula; 8. Ensembles & their correspondence with thermodynamics potentials; 9. Fluctuations; 10. Idea (non-interacting) systems; 11. Distribution functions; 12. Electrolytes; 13. Hot topics;
