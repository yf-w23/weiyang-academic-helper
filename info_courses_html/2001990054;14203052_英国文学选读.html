课程内容简介：
本课程名称为“英国文学选读”，包含英国本土及爱尔兰文学在内。课程将覆盖从维多利亚时期文学、浪漫主义时期文学到二十世纪现代英国文学，包含不同的文学风格，也会涉及到英国的社会、政治和文化历史，以及社会历史情形下的思想观念，学习的重点是小说和诗歌。浪漫主义时期涌现的作者创作主题包括生活、爱情及自然，华兹华斯、科勒律治、济慈是其中杰出代表。维多利亚时期从十九世纪中叶到二十世纪初，代表作品有丁尼生的抒情诗、狄更斯、勃朗苔姐妹、哈代的小说，以及萧伯纳的社会讽刺剧。现代英国文学涉及到死亡和幻灭主题，以尝试新文学形式为特点，包括叶芝、乔伊斯及劳伦斯等人的作品。 课程设置旨在通过原著研读帮助学习者初步体验英国经典文学中表现的思想情感，培养和发展一些基本的小说、诗歌阅读欣赏技能。
Course Description
进度安排：

Weekly Schedule for Selected Readings of English Literature

 

Week 1  

Orientation

Introduction to the arrangement and requirements of the course; global introduction to the general history of English literature and the most prominent features and figures of each period

Key points: What are the prominent features of the literature in the three periods respectively? (Romantic, Victorian, and the 20th century periods)

 

Romantic Period

Week 2  

William Blake (1757-1827): a prophet and mystic in poetry, London, Tyger (Songs of Innocence and Songs Experience);

Robert Burns（1759-1796）: a bard of Scots nationalism, A Red Red Rose; Scots, Wha Hae?

Key points: How do you understand the image of tiger in Black’s poem?  

          What techniques are used to express the emotion of love in A Red Red Rose?

 

Week 3

William Wordsworth (1770-1850): one of the Lakers, a top Romantic poet, The Daffodils;

Jane Austen (1775-1817): works dealing with country landed gentry with features of clarity, economy and skillful use of dialogue and tight plotting, Emma; (Pride and Prejudice recommended)

Key points:   What are conveyed in the poetry and what skills are applied to achieve it?  

In spite of her faults, Emma shines in the description by the author. How is it achieved?

 

Week 4  National Day holidays

 

Week 5 Lord Byron (1788-1828): When We Two Parted

Samuel Coleridge (1772-1834): The Rime of the Ancient Mariner, the bond between man and nature

  Key points: What feelings are expressed in Byron’s poetry? What theme is conveyed in Coleridge’s The Rime of the Ancient Mariner?

 

Week 6  

John Keats (1795-1821): Ode to the Nightingale; Ode on the Grecian Urn

Key points: What is the concept of beauty conveyed by Keat’s poems?  

 

Victorian Period

Week 7  

William Thackeray (1811-1863) : Vanity Fair;

Alfred Tennyson (1809-1892): Mariana

  Key points:  What is the character of Becky Sharp?

How are the themes conveyed in Tennyson’s poem?

 

Week 8

Charles Dickens (1812-1889): a prolific writer always close to the readers, a genius of his time

 Oliver Twist, critical realist work exposing the extreme brutality and corruption of the oppressors under the mask of philanthropy and the hardships and sufferings of the poor; A Visit to Newgate

  Key points: How is the figure of a child used to reveal Dickens’ views on society? What is the mask of philanthropy in Oliver Twist? What are the themes of A Visit to Newgate?

 

Week 9

Emily Bronte (1818-1848): Wuthering Heights which deals with good and evil, imbued with an elemental primitive force

Key points: What are the good and what are the evil in Wuthering Heights?  

 

 

Week 10

Bernard Shaw (1856-1950): Mrs Warren’s Profession

Thomas Hardy (1840-1928): one of the outstanding realists and the last of the great Victorians, a pervasive theme of man’s futile struggle against ineluctable cosmic forces, The Darkling Thrush

   Key points: What is the major conflict between Vivie and her mother, Mrs. Warren?

How is the desolation of the 19th century and uncertainty of 20th century reflected in the poem?

 

Week 11  

Thomas Hardy: Tess of the D’Urberville’s, A Pure Woman Faithfully Presented, a challenge to the conventions of Victorian age

Key points:  What are the causes of Tess’ tragedy? How would you comment on Angel’s personality and his role in Tess’ tragic ending?

 

Twentieth Century

Week 12

William Yeat (1865-1939): one of the founder-figures of modern poetry, The Wild Swans at Coole; Sailing to Byzantium

Key points:  How are the themes conveyed in the two poems?

 

Week 13

Edward Forster (1879-1970): A Passage to India

Key point:   What is the misunderstanding between the English visitors and the local Indians? What are the possible reasons?

 

Week 14

James Joyce (1882-1941): Eveline, (Ulysses, a book with technical innovation and experimentation which carries many of the features that are central to twentieth century literature) (recommended)

     Key points: What are the conflicts that torture Eveline so much?

 

Week 15

   D. H. Lawrence (1885-1930): Odour of Chrysanthemums; (Lady Chatterley’s Lover, belief in sexuality as a   driving force in human relationships) (recommended)

   Key points: What are the reflections of Elizabeth Bates on a marriage? What are the major themes of Lawrence’s works?

 

Week 16  Test
