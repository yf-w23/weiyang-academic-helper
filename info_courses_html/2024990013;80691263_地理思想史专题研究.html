课程简介：
本课程系统追溯从古希腊至近代早期西方地理思想中环境观、自然观的演变，地理学的史志传统与数学传统之争，目的论与自然设计的演化，托勒密《地理学》与普遍地理思想的起源，中世纪的神创宇宙论与唯一居住世界，地理大发现的游记、制图与宇宙志传统，直到启蒙运动后神圣秩序的瓦解，科学革命与机械论的兴起等等。同时也可能对诸如托勒密“居住世界”与中国的“华夷天下观”，希腊星占地理学与中国的星土分野说，以及中西比较的视域下环境决定论与人地关系加以比较。课程旨在引导学生超越单一的思想史谱系，理解地理知识如何深刻塑造了各自的世界观和文化秩序，从而为学生在专业领域内进行理论创新提供广阔的历史视野与哲学基础。
英文课程简介：
Anchored by Clarence Glacken’s seminal work, Traces on the Rhodian Shore, this seminar interrogates the intellectual genealogy of Western geographical thought from Classical Antiquity to the Early Modern era. The course systematically examines the evolving conceptions of nature and environment, highlighting the dialectic between the chorographic (descriptive) and mathematical traditions, the trajectory of teleology and natural design, and the transition from the Ptolemaic Oikoumene and medieval theocentric cosmologies to the mechanistic philosophy of the Scientific Revolution. A distinctive feature of this course is its comparative framework. It juxtaposes Western concepts with East Asian intellectual traditions, exploring parallels between Ptolemy’s Inhabited World and the Chinese Hua-Yi (Sino-Barbarian) order, as well as Greek astro-geography versus the Chinese Fenye (field allocation) system. By situating environmental determinism and human-environment relations within a cross-cultural context, the course encourages students to transcend singular historiographies. The ultimate objective is to provide students with the historical depth and philosophical rigor necessary for theoretical innovation, demonstrating how geographical knowledge has fundamentally structured worldviews and cultural orders across civilizations.
