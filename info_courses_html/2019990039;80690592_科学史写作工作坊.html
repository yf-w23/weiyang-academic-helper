课程简介：
本课程为研究生写作课, 目的是为在读研究生提供英文学术论文写作指导。通过工作坊的形式，本课程的主要目的是帮助学生去修改学期论文 (term paper), 会议论文(conference paper), 论文章节 (chapter), 报告 (presentation/talk), 或任何未发布的研究类型文章 (research article)。由于每个学生的研究和写作进度不一，因此本课程不会订立一个固定修订标准和投稿日期。不过，通过完成课程指定的一系列写作任务，学生将有效把学期论文质量提升，并一定程度克服对英语学术写作的心理焦虑。
英文课程简介：
This is a workshop-style course to help graduate students develop and revise graduate-level research writing assignments, such as term paper, conference paper, chapter, presentation/talk, or any unpublished research article. This course provides the guidance and training for improving the quality of students’ research writing and make them one step closer to the submission standard of top-tier English academic journals. Particularly designed for graduate students in the History and Philosophy of Science, this course aims to help students develop writing skills to craft one’s research in the form of publishable article and help them overcome any anxiety about English academic publishing.

进度安排：

 

Schedule 

Assignment/in-class exercise 

Week 1

Introduction: Go through the syllabus, class arrangement, set writing goals 

Read Shore, Introduction 

Week 2

What is a journal? What is an article? What processes do journal articles go through? 

Read Belcher, Introduction  

-in-class exercise#1: What writing goals do you set to achieve in 16 weeks? 

Week 3

Designing a writing schedule  

Read Shore, chap 3 “How to Write”; Belcher, Week 1 “Designing your plan for writing”

Week 4

Finding a research question

Assignment 1: Write a draft research question 

Week 5

 

Advancing your argument 

Read Belcher, Week 2 “Designing your plan for writing”

-in-class exercise#2: test out your argument 

Week 6

 

Organizing your article around your argument

Assignment 2: Write revised question and a review your article for argument 

Week 7

 

What is an abstract? 

Read Belcher, Week 3 “Abstracting your article” 

-in-class exercise#3: read classmates’ abstracts and draft your own; get feedback from classmates and revise your abstract

Week 8

 

Finding suitable academic journals.

Read Belcher, Week 4 “Selecting a Journal” 

-in-class exercise#4: search and read journals to prepare for submission 

Week 9

 

Primary & Secondary sources: Do you understand the differences?

Read Belcher, Week 5 “Refining Your Works Cited” 

 

Week 10

 

How to summarize or paraphrase others’ research 

Assignment 3: Write or revise your related-literature review for your article 

 

Week 11

Claiming research significance

Read Belcher, Week 6 “Crafting Your Claims for Significance” 

Assignment 4: Write and insert your claims for significance.

Week 12

Analyzing and revising evidence

Read Belcher, Week 7 “Analyzing Your Evidence” 

 

Week 13

Types of evidence: quality, relevance, placement

Read Belcher, Week 8 “Presenting Your Evidence” 

 

Week 14

Strengthening the structure of your article 

Assignment 5: Outline a published article and make a publishable outline for your article. 

Week 15

Writing clear and powerful Opening and Conclusions

Read Belcher, Week 10 “Opening and Concluding Your Article” 

 

Week 16 

Editing your sentences 

Assignment 6: Revise your article using self-diagnostic kit (strengthen verbs; clarify pronouns; decrease prepositions, cut unnecessary words)
