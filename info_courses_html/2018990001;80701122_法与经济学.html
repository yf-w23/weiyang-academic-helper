课程简介：
法与经济学这个学科的诞生，大致以1958年芝加哥大学创办《法与经济学杂志》（“Journal of Law and Economics”）和1960年科斯发表《社会成本问题》一文为标志。法与经济学属于一个应用经济学的新领域，它使用经济学的分析工具，对法律与制度领域问题进行效率与社会福利分析，可以为法律问题与政策制定提供很好的参考或决策建议。 法与经济学自诞生以来，已经对法律学术与实践的诸多方面产生了非常广泛的影响。正如耶鲁大学法学院前院长安东尼.科朗曼所说，“在20世纪最后的25年里，对法学产生最大影响的学术思潮，非法与经济学莫属”。法与经济学这个学科发展出来的丰富方法与理论，对分析新法律条文的效率与社会福利，对评价和判断如反垄断、知识产权、金融监管、侵权法等领域的具体案例，都能提供强有力的帮助。 本课为研究生课程，也对高年级本科生开放。课程将对法与经济学学科进行一个较全面的介绍，并且对一些前沿研究领域做更有针对性的重点阐述。本课程将从理论和实证两个方面入手，介绍法与经济学领域的新进展。教师在讲授过程中，将兼顾Chicago School Law&Economics和Price Theory 传统，强调模型建构与模型创新，并鼓励学生思考与创造新的分析模型，最终旨在帮助学生发展与提高学术研究能力。 本课程所覆盖的内容包括：科斯关于交易成本、社会成本的论述，及其与最新区块链技术的可能联系；最优的侵权法规则设计；财产权的经济学原理；合同法与契约设计；知识产权的设计与经济学分析；最优犯罪惩罚与实施力度；反垄断；监管理论，监管制度改革，金融监管；法与经济学的经验研究方法，基于经济学的比较法律分析等。 课程的形式采用讲授与讨论相结合，还将适量安排助教答疑辅导课，帮助学生熟悉经济分析软件的使用。学期大作业要求完成一篇研究性学术论文或报告，占该课程考查的主要部分。课程鼓励学生组成学习小组，最好是跨学科分组，以便于对课程内容进行充分讨论，进行跨学科互助学习，并一起合作完成研究论文。 课程主页： http://danxiaxie.gz01.bdysite.com/dxLawEcon.htm
英文课程简介：
Law & Economics, as a young branch of economics, was formally established when the University of Chicago started the “Journal of Law and Economics” in 1958, and when Ronald Coase published his important article “The Problem of Social Cost” in 1960. Law & Economics has had important impact on the academic and practice of law. A former dean of the Yale Law School, Anthony Kronman, has pointed out that "the intellectual movement that has had the greatest influence on American academic law in the past quarter-century [of the 20th Century]" is Law & economics. The techniques of Law & Economics is useful for analyzing the efficiency of legal rules, and for evaluating legal cases in fields such as antitrust and patent laws. This class will provide a review of the law and economics literature, with a focus on topics and techniques that are at the frontier of research. The course will cover both theory and empirical papers. It will cover, among other things, the following topics: transaction cost, optimal tort rules, the economic consequences of property rights, intellectual property rights (IPR), optimal criminal rules and the effect of police and punishment on crime, antitrust, financial regulation, settlement and plea bargaining, rules vs. standards, the choice between rulemaking and adjudication in administrative law, and empirical research methods in law & economics. The class will be lecture-style, as opposed to Socratic Method, or casual discussion. Regarding regular assignments, students are required to write referee reports for some new research papers in the fields. Students are also required to write and submit a research paper as the main basis for the course score. Students are encouraged to form study groups to discuss, to referee and to write papers together. Students should have the equivalent of an undergraduate economics degree or have taken intermediate microeconomics and calculus.
