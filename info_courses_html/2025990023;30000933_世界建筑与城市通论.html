课程内容简介：
《世界建筑与城市通论》旨在为学生构建一个理解人类建成环境演变与未来的全球性框架。课程内容上将涵盖建筑史与城市史的重要部分，从美索不达米亚、古希腊罗马建筑艺术的源起开始，到文艺复兴、再到工业革命和现代主义等，强调不同文化背景下对空间的思考与应对，教授主要思想、重要建筑案例和相应的技术发展、文化背景等；引申对当代气候变化与全球化等挑战的探讨，并注重阐释中国等非西方文明在建筑与城市上的独特贡献与应对智慧。在方法论上，课程强调跨学科整合，融合建筑史、城市规划、园林与环境科学等多维度视角，通过经典理论研读、经典案例比较以及中国国情的专题研讨，培养学生深度的批判性思维与分析能力。
Course Description
This course aims to construct a global framework for students to understand the evolution and future of the human-built environment. In terms of content, it covers key parts of architectural history and urban history, tracing from the origins of architectural art in Mesopotamia, ancient Greece and Rome, through the Renaissance, to the Industrial Revolution and Modernism. It teaches core ideas, pivotal architectural cases, as well as corresponding technological developments and cultural contexts. The course further extends to reflections on contemporary challenges such as climate change, smart cities and globalization, with an emphasis on interpreting the unique contributions and adaptive wisdom of non-Western civilizations (represented by China) in architecture and urban development. Methodologically, the course stresses interdisciplinary integration, incorporating multi-dimensional perspectives from architectural history, urban planning, landscape architecture and environmental science. Through the study of classic theories, comparative analysis of typical cases, and thematic discussions on China’s national context, it cultivates students’ in-depth critical thinking and analytical abilities.
