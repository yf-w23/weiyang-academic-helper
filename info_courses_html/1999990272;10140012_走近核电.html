课程内容简介：
该课程首先介绍了核能产生的基本原理，揭示巨大核能的来源；然后介绍核电的起源和发展、核电的国际国内现状、以及核电在我国能源结构中的地位，核能发电的原理、并从反应堆物理概念引出了核电众多堆型的由来和特点，核电站组成（一回路系统与二回路系统）与核电的发展历程；接着以华龙一号压水堆为例介绍了核反应堆物理热工特性、设备和结构、以及核电站安全专设系统、仪控系统和辅助系统，核安全要求与核安全监管，引出了与确保核安全有关的挑战和要求；又介绍了核燃料循环，以及不同核电站使用的核燃料和材料，解释了核电有助于缓解气候变化的能力，核废料的管理和处置，引出了核电对环保和“双碳”目标的影响；还介绍了重大核事故的产生原因和影响，引入核安全文化的概念；对聚变堆的原理和国内外进展进行介绍的同时，本课程综合评估当前和潜在的未来核能系统，在考虑工程、经济和社会等方面因素影响情况下提出了未来核能发展展望。 课程安排一次参观清华大学核能技术设计研究院昌平反应堆基地。 本课程将采用讲课和讨论的教学形式。三次大作业和两次小作业，大作业包括课堂讨论和作业写作两部分，教师提供指导和点评，使学生在语言表达能力和文字表达能力都也得到锻炼和提高。
Course Description
This course first introduces the basic principles of nuclear energy generation. It then covers the origin and development of nuclear power, the current situation of nuclear power internationally and domestically, the role of nuclear power in China's energy structure, the principles of nuclear power generation, and the derivation and characteristics of various reactor types from the concept of reactor physics. The course also introduces the components of a nuclear power plant (primary and secondary loop systems) and the development process of nuclear power. Next, taking the Hualong One pressurized water reactor as an example, it explains the physical and thermal characteristics of nuclear reactors, equipment and structures, as well as dedicated safety systems, instrumentation and control systems, and auxiliary systems of nuclear power plants, nuclear safety requirements, and nuclear safety supervision, leading to discussions on the challenges and requirements associated with ensuring nuclear safety. The course also covers the nuclear fuel cycle, the nuclear fuels and materials used in different nuclear power plants, explains how nuclear power contributes to mitigating climate change, and addresses the management and disposal of nuclear waste, highlighting the impact of nuclear power on environmental protection and the 'dual carbon' goals. Additionally, it introduces the causes and impacts of major nuclear accidents and introduces the concept of nuclear safety culture. While discussing the principles of fusion reactors and the progress both domestically and internationally, the course provides a comprehensive assessment of current and potential future nuclear energy systems and proposes prospects for the development of nuclear energy considering engineering, economic, and social factors. Presenting and writing，followed by in-class discussions, will improve students’ oral and writing ability. Guidance and comments provided.
