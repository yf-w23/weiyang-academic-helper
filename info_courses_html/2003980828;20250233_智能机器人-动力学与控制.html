Course Description
The course focuses on the four basic problems of kinematics, dynamics, planning, and control in robotics. These problems are discussed from problem posing, mathematical modeling, and algorithm implementation perspectives. Through many problem analyses and practice, the panorama of robotics is finally established, the basic theory of robotics is understood, and the basic skills of modeling, analysis, and solution in modern robotics are mastered. In part on kinematics and dynamics, the course will discuss basic concepts and analytical methods and introduce numerical methods that are more suitable for the application. In the planning part, we will focus on states of art applications that use optimization and intelligent algorithms. In the control part, the single-task control in joint space, single-task control in operation space, and multi-task control in operation space are systematically discussed as a process. The robot's intelligence is improved by properly integrating prediction, optimization, and learning algorithms.
