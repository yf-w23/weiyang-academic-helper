课程内容简介：
《环境化学》是环境学科的重要基础课程，综合运用各种化学知识探究和了解环境介质中化学物质的存在、转化及其造成的环境效应，本课程分为大气环境化学和水环境化学两部分。大气环境化学主要介绍对流层微量化学污染物如何在太阳光下反应从而造成光化学烟雾、酸雨和颗粒物污染，以及平流层臭氧破坏和南极臭氧空洞周期性出现的科学机制。水环境化学部分主要介绍大气二氧化碳存在下的碳酸盐平衡、金属离子的配位平衡和沉淀-溶解平衡、水中颗粒物的特性，以及天然水体中有机物水解、还原和光化学降解的机理。 通过《环境化学》课程的学习，既掌握了过去或现有大气、水环境中化学物质的转化机制以及污染成因，又能养成“环境化学”的整体思维，即针对某一环境污染问题，首先要了解造成该环境污染问题的主要化学物质及其来源（清单、排放规律），进一步了解这些主要污染物质在相关环境介质中的分配、转化机制，为科学合理地控制和解决该污染问题提供精准的策略。
Course Description
Environmental Chemistry is an important foundation course for the environmental science discipline, which integrates various chemical knowledge to explore and understand the existence, transformation, and environmental effects of chemical substances in the environmental medium. The course is divided into two parts: atmospheric environmental chemistry and aquatic environmental chemistry. Atmospheric environmental chemistry mainly introduces the scientific mechanisms of how trace chemical pollutants in the troposphere react under sunlight to cause photochemical smog, acid rain, and particulate matter pollution, as well as the periodic appearance of the ozone hole in the stratosphere and the scientific mechanism of ozone destruction in the Antarctic. The aquatic environmental chemistry section mainly introduces the carbonate equilibrium under atmospheric carbon dioxide, the coordination equilibrium of metal ions, the precipitation-dissolution equilibrium, the characteristics of particles in water, and the mechanisms of organic matter hydrolysis, reduction, and photochemical degradation in natural water bodies. Through the study of Environmental Chemistry, students not only acquire the transformation mechanisms and pollution causes of chemical substances in the past or current atmospheric and water environments, but also cultivate a holistic thinking of "environmental chemistry," i.e., when facing a specific environmental pollution problem, one should first understand the main chemical substances that cause the problem and their sources (list, emission patterns), and then understand the distribution and transformation mechanisms of these main pollutants in relevant environmental media, so as to provide precise strategies for scientifically and rationally controlling and solving the pollution problem.
