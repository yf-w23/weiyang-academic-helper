课程内容简介：
日语第二外语（1）是面向非日语专业学生的初级课程。教材选取本校教师编写的第二外语专用教材《新世纪日本语教程-初级》。本课程每次2学时，每周2次，16周学期内课堂用时64课时，完成教材第1-12课的字母、发音、单词、语法、课文、会话、练习等各项内容。其中日语字母学习较为复杂，占全课程的三分之一。语法学习分为句型与词尾变化两大类。句型主要需要掌握：名词判断句、形容词描写句、动词动宾句、存在句四种形式。词尾变化主要掌握：判断助动词、形容词、形容动词的各种变化规律；以及动词第一连用形和第二连用形。 课堂时间教师讲解语言知识和情境操练并重。会话练习主要采用教材中的对话训练。造句练习以教师自编PPT教程为主，配合语法讲解，注重语法上的针对性及话题内容上的启发性。单词记忆结合猜谜语等游戏进行，授课过程中伴有文化讲解。课下作业初级阶段以强化训练为主，期中考试后增加自主学习等启发式内容。力争在初级阶段为学生尽可能打下良好语法基础、提高应用的熟练度、进一步引发学习日语了解日本的持久兴趣，使第二外语的学习能够指向一个认知新领域。
Course Description
Japanese as a Second Foreign Language (1) is a beginner-level course designed for non-Japanese major students. The course utilizes the specialized textbook "New Century Japanese Course - Beginner" written by our school's instructors. Each session of this course consists of 2 academic hours, held twice a week, totaling 64 class hours over a 16-week semester to cover Units 1-12 of the textbook, including alphabets, pronunciation, vocabulary, grammar, texts, conversations, and exercises. The study of Japanese alphabets, which is relatively complex, comprises one-third of the entire course. Grammar study is divided into sentence patterns and word ending changes. The main sentence patterns to be mastered include noun judgment sentences, adjective descriptive sentences, verb-object sentences, and existential sentences. Word ending changes mainly cover the rules for judgment auxiliary verbs, adjectives, adjectival verbs, as well as the first and second conjunctive forms of verbs. During class time, language knowledge explanation and situational practice are emphasized. Conversation practice primarily involves dialogues from the textbook. Sentence construction exercises are mainly conducted through teacher-prepared PowerPoint presentations, complemented by grammar explanations focusing on targeted grammar and inspiring topics. Vocabulary memorization is combined with games such as riddles, and cultural explanations are integrated into the teaching process. Homework assignments in the beginner stage mainly focus on reinforcement training, with the addition of self-directed learning activities post-midterm exam. The goal is to lay a solid foundation in grammar, enhance application proficiency, stimulate long-term interest in learning Japanese and understanding Japan during the beginner stage, thereby directing the study of the second foreign language towards exploring a new cognitive domain.
