课程简介：
本课程系统介绍物理仿真的经典知识和研究前沿，为对计算机图形学、具身智能、人工智能驱动的科学研究、生成式模型感兴趣的同学提供下列研究基础：1）理解基础的力学和应用数学工具，建立物理和数学直觉；2）掌握现代的高性能编程工具（Taichi Lang/NVIDIA Warp）；3）了解学科前沿的开放问题，培养学术志趣和研究审美。 课程第一部分讲解仿真基础算法，介绍刚体，软体、流体等常见物理系统的经典处理方法。课程第二部分讨论物理仿真中的进阶技术，包括多物理系统、多重网格（Multigrids）、实时仿真等内容。课程第三部分介绍仿真中的前沿课题，包括生成式仿真、神经网络求解偏微分方程（AI for PDE）、具身智能、软体机器人等研究前沿并基于近年的论文不定期更新。课程第四部分组织学生进行项目展示并评选最佳课程项目。往年优秀学生项目参见https://people.iiis.tsinghua.edu.cn/~taodu/teaching/course_info.pdf 本课程欢迎理工科专业本科生和研究生选课，往年学生来自计算机、机械、数学、力学、航空航天等不同专业。本课程为选课学生积极创造科研和实习机会，往年学生曾在课程支持下访问海外知名图形学实验室开展学术交流与合作（Stanford UGVR/CMU/UCSD/MIT)、在计算机顶级学术会议上发表论文（ICLR & NeurIPS）、获得院系学术奖项（致理书院青藜学术一等奖）和知名企业奖学金（商汤奖学金）、以及进入顶尖游戏工作室实习（腾讯光子工作室）。
英文课程简介：
This course offers a holistic introduction to the classical knowledge and research frontiers of physical simulation. It provides the following research foundations for students interested in computer graphics, embodied intelligence, AI for science, and generative models: 1) Understanding fundamental mechanics and applied mathematical tools, building physical and mathematical intuition; 2) Developing coding skills for modern high-performance programming languages (Taichi Lang/NVIDIA Warp); 3) Understanding open problems at the forefront of the discipline and cultivating academic interests and research aesthetics. This course consists of four parts. Part I (simulation basics) explains classic techniques for simulating rigid-body, soft-body, cloth, and fluid systems. Part II (advanced techniques) introduces advanced simulation methods for multi-physics, large-scale, and real-time applications. Part III (research frontier) discusses the latest research topics in physics simulation with regular updates from recent papers in AI for Science, generative models, and Embodied AI. Part IV (workshop) invites all students to present their course projects and selects the Best Course Project award. We welcome undergraduate and graduate students in all STEM (science, technology, engineering, and mathematics) majors to take our course. Past years' courses have enrolled students majoring in computer sciences, mechanical engineering, mathematics, mechanics, aeronautics, etc. This course also offers potential research and internship opportunities in computer graphics. In previous years, students visited or collaborated with graphics labs at prestigious universities (Stanford UGVR/CMU/UCSD/MIT), published at a top-tier computer science conference, won academic awards or industrial scholarships, and interned at world-renowned industrial labs.
