课程内容简介：
课程从时间和空间两个维度探究印度文明，思考印度的“李约瑟之问”。从考古角度看，印度河文明是印度文明的起源。从文献角度看，《梨俱吠陀》是现存最早的印度文明证据。印度是语言的博物馆，古代和近代的语言体现了文明的多样性。阿育王时期的印度北部有过短暂的统一，阿育王本人也成为多种宗教文献中的理想统治者。中世纪印度教的印度，王国迭起，权力中心此起彼伏。印度教、佛教、耆那教等宗教是印度人对世界宗教文化的创造，也影响了印度古典文学、艺术和建筑，天文学和数学。 公元11世纪开始，穆斯林、锡克人、莫卧尔人和英国人在南亚次第登场，最终在民族主义运动中印度成为了印度人的印度。印度与西方，印度与中国，是印度文明与外部世界发生的主要关联。纵观印度文明的发展历程，可以看出其具有宗教性、多样性、包容性等三大特点。本课程探寻从印度河文明到近现代的政治社会、宗教哲学、语言文学、艺术，探讨印度文明的多样性包容性，也探索未来世界中印文明互鉴的可能。
Course Description
The course explores Indian civilization in both its temporal and spatial dimensions, to think about the Needham Puzzle in India. From an archaeological perspective, the Indus civilization is the origin of Indian civilization. From a documentary perspective, the Rig Veda is the earliest surviving evidence of Indian civilization. India is a museum of languages, reflecting the diversity of civilizations. There was a brief period of unification in northern India under Ashoka, who himself became the ideal ruler in the literature of many religions. Medieval Hindu India saw a succession of kingdoms and centers of power. Religions such as Hinduism, Buddhism and Jainism were Indian creation of the world and also influenced classical Indian literature, art and architecture, astronomy and mathematics, etc. From the 11th century AD, Muslims, Sikhs, Mughals and British appeared in South Asia, and eventually India became an India for Indians in a nationalist movement. India and the West, India and China, were the main connections that took place between Indian civilization and the outside world. A glance at the development of Indian civilization reveals three main characteristics: its religious nature, its diversity and its inclusiveness. This course explores the political and social, religious and philosophical, linguistic and literary, and artistic aspects of Indian civilization from the Indus civilization to modern times, and explores the diversity and inclusiveness of Indian civilization, and a possibility of mutual Learning between Chinese and Indian Civilizations in the Future World.
