课程内容简介：
This course aims to provide an overview of a broad range of topics in astrophysics. The following chapters will be discussed: 1. Basic properties of stars 2. Physical States of the Stellar Interior 3. Binary Stars 4. The Life and Death of the Stars 5. Cosmic Fireworks 6. The Solar System 7. Interstellar Matter 8. The Galaxies Modern astronomical observing techniques closely related to these topics will also be briefly discussed. Reference text book (optional): An Introduction to Modern Astrophysics - Bradley W. Carroll, Dale A. Ostlie
Course Description
进度安排：

Chapter 1: Properties of Stars

Observational properties: 

Brightness, color, spectrum, and distribution

Interpretation 

Mass & Star Formation (Jeans Length & Mass, Virial Theorem)

Luminosity (Boltzmann Eqn, Planck’s Law, Blackbody) and Temperature

Size (Stefan-Boltzmann Law)

Composition

Hertzsprung-Russell Diagram (Luminosity–Temperature)

Globular and Open Clusters

How to Measure?

 

Chapter 2: Physical State of the Stellar Interior

Stellar Structure

Conservation of Mass (dm/dr)

Conservation of Energy (dL/dr)

Eqn of Hydrostatic Equilibrium (Gravity .vs. a Pressure-Gradient Force, dP/dr)

Eqn of Energy Transport (dT/dr)

Nuclear Reactions

H-burning (pp Chain, CN Cycle) and He-burning

Structure of Main-Sequence Stars

 

Chapter 3: Binary Stars

Classifications (Spectroscopic, Eclipsing, Close)

Mass Function

 

Chapter 4: The Life and Death of the Stars

Evolution of Low-Mass Stars

Pre-Main-Sequence

Core H-Burning

Red Giant

Helium Flash

Asymptotic Giant Branch

White Dwarf and Chandrasekhar Mass Limit

Evolution of High-Mass Stars

 

Chapter 5: Cosmic Fireworks

CATASTROPHES

Thermonuclear Supernova (Exploding White Dwarf)

Core-Collapse Supernova (Core-Collapse of Massive Stars)

Gamma-ray Burst

After the death of stars

Supernova remnant

Compact objects: White dwarf, Neutron star,  and Black hole

 

Chapter 6: The Solar System

The Sun as a main-sequence star

The environment of the solar system

Planets and moons: motions

Planet and moons: physical properties

Minor planets

 

Chapter 7: Interstellar Matter

The interstellar extinction curve

Optical and physical properties of dust grains

Emission by dust grains

Implications for grain composition

 

Chapter 8: The Galaxies

The Milky Way system and the Local Group 

Galaxy clusters and large-scale structure

Stellar, gas, and dust content in galaxies of different types

A brief introduction to galaxy dynamics

The rotation curve of galaxies and the dark matter

The heart of galaxies: Supermassive black holes and active galactic nuclei

The intergalactic medium and the reionization of the Universe

Gravitational lensing

The formation of stars and galaxies at high redshifts
