课程内容简介：
这门课帮助学生从政治哲学的视角来认识并反思我们生活于其中的空间（特别是建筑）和我们日用而不自知的技术，进而思考政治哲学的抽象理论与日常生活的具体实践之间的关联。课程前半部分关注技术与政治的关系，后半部分则从政治哲学的视角认识空间与建筑。我们将思考空间与技术如何体现着人们对正义、平等、自由等政治价值的理解和追求，又如何影响着这些价值的实现。本课程尤其适合人文、社科、工程、建筑、美术、设计等不同专业背景的学生修读，学生既可以收获自己专业领域以外的新知，又能够从新的视角看待自己所学的专业。
Course Description
This course helps students to, from the perspective of political theory, understand and reflect on the spaces (especially architectures) we inhabit and the technologies we use on a daily basis without much critical awareness. It thereby invites students to consider how abstract theories of political philosophy are relevant for the concrete practices of our everyday life. The first half of the course will focus on the relationship between technology and politics, whereas the second half will allow us to look at space and architecture through the lens of political theory. Throughout the semester, we will consider how space and technology embody people’s understandings and pursuits of key political values such as justice, equality, and liberty on the one hand, and how they influence the realization of these values on the other. This course is especially suitable for students majoring in humanities, social sciences, engineering, architecture, fine art, and design. By studying this course, students from different backgrounds will both acquire new knowledge from outside their fields of study and learn to look at their fields of study from a new perspective.
