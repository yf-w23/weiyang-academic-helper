课程内容简介：
理论计算机科学是计算机科学的核心领域之一，致力于以数学方法深入研究计算、信息处理与逻辑推理等基础性问题。本课程旨在系统介绍该领域的基本概念、核心方法与代表性成果，以“逻辑与计算”的关系为主线，帮助学生构建对计算本质的理解，领略计算理论的逻辑之美与独特的思维方式。 课程内容围绕五大模块展开，覆盖理论计算机科学中最重要且相辅相成的研究方向： 计算模型：介绍 λ 演算、图灵机模型、丘奇-图灵论题与克林递归定理，探讨哥德尔不完备定理，为理解“何为可计算”及其边界奠定理论基础； 计算复杂性理论：系统讲解时间复杂性与空间复杂性，分析 P、NP、PSPACE、PH 等基本复杂性类，深入介绍库克-列文定理与 NP 完全性规约方法； 随机性与计算：介绍随机算法与复杂性类 BPP，探讨其与确定性计算模型的关系，并引入去随机化的基本思想，揭示随机性在计算过程中的关键作用； 密码学基础：讲解伪随机生成器、零知识证明等现代密码学中的核心概念，展示复杂性理论在密码学安全性分析中的应用； 逻辑与计算：从 λ 演算与直觉主义自然演绎出发，探讨命题即类型（Curry-Howard 对应）等基本范式，揭示形式逻辑与程序设计之间的深层联结。 本课程注重理论思维与形式化推理能力的培养，旨在帮助学生夯实理论基础、提升面向计算机科学的数学抽象能力，并为今后在算法设计、复杂性理论、编程语言理论、形式验证与密码学等领域的深入学习打下坚实基础。
Course Description
Theoretical computer science is one of the core areas of computer science. It is dedicated to the in-depth study of computation, information processing, and logical reasoning using mathematical methods. This course aims to systematically introduce the fundamental concepts, core methods, and representative results of this field. It takes the relationship between “logic and computation” as its central theme, helping students build an understanding of the essence of computation and appreciate the logical elegance and distinctive thinking patterns of theoretical computer science. The course is organized around five major modules, covering the most important and complementary research directions in theoretical computer science: Models of Computation: Introduction to the λ-calculus, Turing machine model, Church-Turing thesis, and Kleene's recursion theorem; exploration of Gödel’s incompleteness theorems; Computational Complexity Theory: A systematic study of time and space complexity; in-depth introduction to the Cook-Levin theorem and NP-completeness reductions; Randomness and Computation: Introduction to randomized algorithms and the complexity class BPP; discussion of the relationship between randomized and deterministic models of computation; basic techniques in derandomization and the critical role of randomness in computation; Foundations of Cryptography: Coverage of core concepts such as pseudorandom generators and zero-knowledge proofs in modern cryptography; Logic and Computation: λ-calculus and intuitionistic natural deduction, the Curry-Howard correspondence. This course emphasizes the cultivation of theoretical thinking and formal reasoning skills. It is designed to help students solidify their theoretical foundation, enhance their mathematical abstraction abilities for computer science, and lay the groundwork for advanced studies in algorithm design, complexity theory, programming language theory, formal verification, and cryptography.
