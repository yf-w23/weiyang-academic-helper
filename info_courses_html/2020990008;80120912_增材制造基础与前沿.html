课程简介：
增材制造（俗称3D打印）是一类颠覆性制造技术的统称。这类技术以三维数字模型为基础，通过喷头挤出、高能束扫描等手段，将金属、陶瓷、树脂等材料基本单元有选择性地连接起来，构建起三维实体。一般情况下，它们采用逐层堆积的方式。相比传统的减材制造和等材制造，增材制造可提供极大的设计自由度和制造能力，可实现多尺度、多材料、多功能的优化和集成。同时，增材制造可以极大地缩短供应链，这对于应对全球供应链断链危机至关重要。因此，增材制造技术快速发展，正深刻变革着航空航天、汽车、国防、化工、医药、能源等领域。本课程将分为三部分。第一部分，将介绍增材制造的基本原理、技术分类、典型应用和未来展望。第二部分，将基于本人的研究经历和体会讲述金属激光增材制造，将从激光束线与金属材料的相互作用出发，介绍原位实时监测技术、熔池行为、结构缺陷、力学行为等。第三部分，将进行实践教学和小组讨论，探寻增材制造的关键科学问题与技术挑战。
英文课程简介：
Additive manufacturing (AM, aka 3D printing) is a collection of disruptive technologies that fabricate parts directly from digital computer-aided design and drafting models, usually layer upon layer. In comparison to conventional subtractive or formative manufacturing, this emerging cluster offers unprecedented design freedom and manufacturing capabilities for multiscale, multimaterial, and multifunctional optimization and integration. In addition, AM requires a short supply chain, which becomes increasingly critical when one considers global supply chain disruption risks. As a result, metal AM has been evolving rapidly during the past decade and is transforming the aeronautic, aerospace, automotive, defense, chemical, medical, and energy industries. In this course, we will first introduce basic AM concepts, technical classification, representative applications, and future outlook. After that, we will talk about operando monitoring techniques, laser-matter interaction, melt pool behavior, solidification behavior, solid phase transformation, structural defects, mechanical behavior, and so forth. In the end, we will conduct some AM experiment and discuss the AM challenges in a group manner.
