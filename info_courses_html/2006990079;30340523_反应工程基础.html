课程内容简介：
《反应工程基础》是化学工程系本科生重要的专业基础课，属必修环节，3学分。面向本科生的反应工程教学的目标是使学生掌握化学反应工程基础知识、研究方法与研究思路，同时了解反应工程最新进展及发展方向，提高创新思维能力。《反应工程基础》教学内容主要分为三个大部分： 一是理想反应器设计原理和反应机理，从通用的摩尔平衡方程出发，针对4种典型的反应器型式建立相应的设计方程，在反应动力学和化学计量关系的基础上，针对等温和非等温过程、简单反应和复杂反应体系，建立严谨的数学模型表达，以解析解和数值解的方式设计所需的反应器，同时教学内容进一步引入反应机理和反应途径的讲述，介绍了前沿科学研究中的生化反应器、膜反应器、微尺度反应器、化学气相沉积（CVD）等反应器的设计原理和发展； 二是非理想流动，在理想反应器的基础上，建立真实反应器的非理想性概念，引入停留时间分布的概念，量化反应器的非理想性，并重点讲述多釜串联模型、离析流模型和扩散模型，建立非理想反应器的科学表达； 三是气固催化动力学基础和主要气固催化反应器的科学基础以及应用实例。
Course Description
"Elements of Reaction Engineering" is an essential undergraduate course for chemical engineering students, constituting a compulsory component with a weight of 3 credit hours. The goal of teaching reaction engineering to undergraduates is to equip students with fundamental knowledge, research methods, and investigative approaches in chemical reaction engineering. Additionally, the course aims to familiarize students with the latest advancements and future directions in reaction engineering, enhancing their innovative thinking capabilities. The instructional content of "Fundamentals of Reaction Engineering" is divided into three major parts. Firstly, it covers the principles of ideal reactor design and reaction mechanisms. Starting from the general mole balance equations, the course establishes corresponding design equations for four typical reactor types. Based on reaction kinetics and chemical stoichiometry, it addresses isothermal and non-isothermal processes, simple and complex reaction systems, developing rigorous mathematical models for reactor design. The content also delves into the explanation of reaction mechanisms and pathways, introducing design principles and developments in biochemical reactors, membrane reactors, micro-scale reactors, and chemical vapor deposition (CVD) reactors from the forefront of scientific research. Secondly, the course explores non-ideal flow. Building upon the foundation of ideal reactors, it introduces the concept of non-ideal characteristics in real reactors. The course incorporates the concept of residence time distribution to quantify the non-ideal nature of reactors. Emphasis is placed on discussing multi-tank series models, plug flow models, and diffusion models, establishing a scientific expression for non-ideal reactor behavior. Thirdly, the course covers the fundamentals of gas-solid catalytic kinetics and the scientific basis of major gas-solid catalytic reactors, along with practical examples of their applications.
