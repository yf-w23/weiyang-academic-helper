课程简介：
中子物理学是紧密伴随着核物理学的发展而成长的，如今已经成为比较成熟的学科，在科学研究、工业应用、国防安全、医疗卫生等领域发挥了不可替代的作用。自从二十世纪末期以来，世界主要科技强国已经建设了包括反应堆源、散裂源在内的多个大型中子源用于科学研究；在过去的十年中，又有大量的微型加速器驱动的中子源加入了这个行列，扩展了研究与应用的范围。在这个背景下，亟需一门课程来对中子探测与测量中的核心物理与技术问题进行系统地介绍。 本课程的总学时为32学时，分三部分进行讨论：（1）与中子产生与探测相关的物理机制与原理，（2）针对中子的探测方法，（3）中子应用时的测量方法。将分别梳理每一部分的第一性原理，从更加基础的角度来开展讨论。在教学方法上，每一部分总学时为10学时，其中课堂讲授为8学时，学生课堂讨论和随堂测验共2学时，另有2次课后习题作业。对于第三部分，还将安排在清华大学微型强子源上的2学时实验（备选实验（1）：慢中子衰减截面与能量的关系曲线；备选实验（2）中子诱发伽马能谱测量与分析；备选实验（3）：位敏中子成像）。课程成绩由课后习题（30%）、课堂讨论（20%）、随堂测验（40%）和实验环节（10%）共同贡献得到。 通过本课程的学习，可使学生掌握中子产生、中子与物质作用的基本原理，了解中子测量的主要方法与特点，为开展中子探测与测量相关的研究工作打下良好基础。
英文课程简介：
The research of neutron physics has played an irreplaceable role in the areas of scientific research, industrial applications, homeland security and medical treatment. More than ten intense neutron sources, including reactor sources, spallation sources have been established all around the world by U.S., France, U.K., E.U., China and Japan in the past decades. The compact accelerator driven neutron sources, which have the lowered neutron flux but are very flexible and can be easily deployed on the campus of the University, can also contribute and may significantly expand the neutron’s applications in a variety of industrial regions. In this context, a course addressing the discussion of fundamental mechanisms and principles of neutron’s detection and measurement thus should be provided to the graduate students before they will start the neutron related study. There are three sections involved in this course. The first section focuses on the discussion of mechanisms of neutron production and the interaction between neutrons and matters. The second section focuses on the principles of neutron detection. And the third section will elaborate the methods for neutron measurement. Each section consists of 8 lecture-hours, 2 discussion & quiz-hours and 2 exercises. For the third section, an experiment conducted on CPHS (compact pulsed hadron source) would also be provided. The candidate experiments could be (1) the measurement of cross sections for slow neutrons; (2) the neutron induced prompt gamma-ray measurement and analysis; (3) the position sensitive neutron imaging.
