课程内容简介：
课程立足我国城市迈入“存量发展”阶段的转型背景，顺应国家“实施城市更新行动”的战 略指引与社会诉求，带领学生探索和了解城市更新的理论发展、制度建设和实践运作知识， 让学生在既有挑战性又有趣味性的系列话题探讨中，完成对城市更新学术前沿和实践进展的 具体学习。“理论维度”上的课程学习内容和讨论议题关注城市更新的理论思潮和热点问 题，主要包括：1）从“推土机”到“有机更新”：城市更新思潮和实践变迁；2）城市更新 的“绅士化”现象：何去何从；3）城市更新的维度：主体-资金-空间-运维；4）“社区规划 师”介入下的城市更新：基层治理变革等。“实践维度”上的课程学习内容和实际案例分析 聚焦国内外更新实践的经验得失，主要包括：1）北京城市更新实践探访：身边的更新改造； 2）解剖经典城市更新案例：来自国际的经验；3）我国城市更新实践解读：进展与反思等。 通过讲授、调查、讨论等多元授课方式，课程将帮助学生建立起正确的城市更新思想观和价 值观，了解和掌握城市更新的理论知识和实践方法等。
Course Description
The course leads students to explore and understand the theories, institutions, and practical operation of urban regeneration, so as to respond to the unique characteristics of Chinese cities entering the “stock development” phase, and meet the national strategy of “implementing the urban regeneration action” and the related social demands in China. Students will study the academic frontiers and practical progress of urban regeneration through discussions on a series of interesting and challenging topics. On the one hand, the main contents and topics based on theoretical exploration of the course include: 1) From "bulldozer" to "organic regeneration": the ideological trends and practical progress of urban regeneration; 2) "Gentrification" of urban regeneration: where to go; 3) Dimensions of urban regeneration: subjects-capital-space-maintenance; 4) Urban regeneration with the intervention of "community planners" : grassroots governance reform, etc. On the other hand, the main contents and case analysis of the course based on practice include: 1) Field study of Beijing urban regeneration cases: transformation around us; 2) Dissecting classic urban regeneration cases: learning from international experience; 3) Practical interpretation of urban regeneration cases in China: progress and reflection. Through various teaching methods, including lectures, discussions, case study, and field investigation, the course aims to expand the cognitive level of students on urban regeneration, and provide them with basic knowledge for engaging in various undertakings related to urban development or construction in the future.
