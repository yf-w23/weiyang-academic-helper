课程简介：
本课程介绍含金属、硼、硅、磷、硫和氟等元素的有机化合物，讨论这些元素有机化合物的成键特点、制备方法、化学性质和用途。此外还介绍该研究领域的最新进展和元素有机化合物在催化有机合成中的应用。 在该课程中重点介绍金属有机化学，包括化合物结构组成，成键特点，基元反应，研究手段及在催化有机合成和材料合成方面的应用。由于碳－金属键独特的反应性能，使得金属有机化合物的反应具有选择性高、条件温和、原子经济性等优点，是实现高效合成的重要手段，成为绿色合成及新材料合成的核心途径之一。
英文课程简介：
This course is intended to introduce students to an exciting, rapidly growing interdisciplinary subject: organotransition-metal chemistry. The chemistry of organotransition-metal complexes has continued to experience explosive growth in novel substances, new reactions, surprising mechanisms, and further applications to both synthesis and industrial processes. It is appropriate to begin by defining this subject. Organotransition metal chemistry is concerned with compounds which have an organic group bonded to a transition metal through a direct metal-carbon bond, either a σ or a π bond. This subject does not include coordination compounds, which are substances having donor bonds from elements other than carbon, such as metal complexes of amines, halide ions, and the like. In practice many important organotransition-metal compounds are also ‘coordination compounds,” but most coordination compounds do not possess direct metal-carbon bonds and are therefore not organotransition-metal compounds. This course is addressed to well-prepared senior chemistry majors and first-year graduate students. It is not comprehensive, but instructive – it is concerned with general principles and is illustrated with practical examples. This course will be of interest to both inorganic and organic students. The former are usually more concerned with structure, spectroscopic properties, and theoretical concepts, whereas the latter are principally interested chemical reactions, including both reaction mechanisms and applications to synthesis. This broad subject requires knowledge from both subfields of chemistry. I attempt to provide and to blend the inorganic and the organic aspects of organotransition-metal chemistry. However, emphasis here is given to reactions, to reaction mechanisms, and particularly to applications to organic synthesis and homogeneous catalysis. I also include industrial applications of organotransition metal chemistry in the homogeneous catalysis.
