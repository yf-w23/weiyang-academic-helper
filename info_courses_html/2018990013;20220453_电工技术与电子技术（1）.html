课程内容简介：
本课程是面向非电类工科专业的国家级精品课程，着力培养学生从事本专业学习和工作中所需的电气工程和控制工程学科的基础知识和基本技能。课程以“电路理论-技术应用-工程实践”为主线，构建了电路理论和电机控制两大教学模块。 电路理论模块系统阐释电能传输与转换的基本规律，涵盖直流电路、正弦交流电路、三相电路、电路的暂态分析等内容。通过引入Multisim等仿真工具，帮助学生掌握节点电位法、叠加定理、戴维宁定理等核心分析方法。结合工程案例和用电安全，将抽象理论与实际应用场景有机融合，巩固所学知识。 电机控制模块着重解析电路理论在电能转化中的具体应用，具体讲授三相异步电动机结构特性、继电器-接触器控制系统设计，以及可编程控制器技术。通过典型工业控制案例的剖析，培养学生搭建逻辑控制系统的工程实践能力，为智能制造、工业自动化等领域的应用奠定基础。 本课程非常注重实践教学环节，使学生感性并深入理解所讲授电路理论的实际意义，提升学生在本专业和行业相关领域解决实际工程问题的核心素养。
Course Description
This nationally recognized elite course for non-electrical engineering majors cultivates fundamental knowledge and practical skills in electrical and control engineering essential for professional development. Structured around the "Circuit Theory-Technical Application-Engineering Practice" framework, the curriculum integrates two core modules: Circuit Theory and Motor Control. The Circuit Theory module systematically explores the principles of electrical energy transmission and conversion, covering DC circuits, sinusoidal AC circuits, three-phase systems, and transient analysis. Through Multisim simulations and analytical methods—including node voltage analysis, superposition theorem, and Thévenin's theorem—students develop mathematical modeling skills. Real-world engineering cases and electrical safety practices bridge theoretical concepts with applications in power distribution and electronic systems. The Motor Control module focuses on the application of circuit theory in electromechanical energy conversion, this module delves into three-phase induction motors, relay-contactor control systems, and Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs). By dissecting industrial automation case studies, students gain hands-on experience in designing logic control systems, equipping them for challenges in smart manufacturing and industrial automation. The course places significant emphasis on practical teaching components, enabling students to gain a tangible and profound understanding of the practical significance of circuit theory. This enhances students' core competencies in solving real-world engineering problems relevant to their majors and related industries.
