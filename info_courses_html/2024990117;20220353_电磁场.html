课程内容简介：
《电磁场》是电气工程所有分支学科的理论基石，也是连接物理原理与工程应用的桥梁。 不同于《电磁学》侧重于定性观察物理现象，也不同于《电动力学》追求微观机制与时空对称的理论统一，本课程立足于工程建模与计算，聚焦于如何利用这些数学模型，在给定的几何结构与边界条件下，求解工程设备的电磁参数，解决绝缘、发热与干扰等实际问题。 课程将以场论为数学工具，通过梯度、散度与旋度建立描述空间场的底层语言。随后遵循从静态到动态的认知路径，由静电场与恒定场入手，解析绝缘设计原理与电阻、电感、电容等电路参数的物理本源。最终延伸至时变电磁场，攻克准静态场建模、集肤效应与邻近效应造成的损耗分析以及电磁兼容设计等复杂工程问题，从而实现理论与实践的融合。 同学们将掌握一套将电磁工程应用抽象为数学模型的方法论，具备分析和解决那些电路理论无法解释的电磁工程问题的核心能力。
Course Description
Electromagnetic Fields serves as the theoretical foundation for all branches of electrical engineering and acts as the bridge connecting physical principles with engineering applications. Distinct from "Electromagnetism," which emphasizes the qualitative observation of physical phenomena, and "Electrodynamics," which seeks the theoretical unification of microscopic mechanisms and space-time symmetry, this course is grounded in engineering modeling and computation. It focuses on utilizing mathematical models within specific geometric structures and boundary conditions to calculate the electromagnetic parameters of equipment and solve practical issues such as insulation breakdown, heating, and interference. The course utilizes field theory as its mathematical tool, establishing the fundamental language for describing spatial fields through gradient, divergence, and curl. Following a cognitive progression from static to dynamic systems, it begins with electrostatic and steady fields to analyze insulation design principles and the physical origins of circuit parameters like resistance, inductance, and capacitance. The curriculum then extends to time-varying electromagnetic fields. It addresses complex engineering challenges—including quasi-static field modeling, loss analysis resulting from skin and proximity effects, and electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) design—to achieve a seamless integration of theory and practice. By the end of this course, students will master a methodology for abstracting engineering applications into mathematical models and acquire the core capability to analyze and solve complex electromagnetic engineering problems that lie beyond the scope of traditional circuit theory.
