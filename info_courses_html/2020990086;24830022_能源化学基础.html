课程内容简介：
《能源化学基础》是面向自强书院同学开设的普化类初级课程。本课程旨在系统讲授能源物质与物质结构基础；化学热力学、动力学与能量转化；氧化还原反应与电化学能源等核心基础理论，并阐释其在能源获取、存储、转换与传输过程中的应用。 讲授内容主要包括： 1. 能源物质与物质结构基础：从能源利用与能量转化的视角，系统讲解原子/分子结构、化学键、晶体结构及物质状态等核心知识，重点阐明不同类型能源物质（如化石燃料、电池电极材料）的“结构-性质”关联，解释其能源应用特性的差异。 2.化学热力学与能量转化方向及限度：讲解化学热力学基本原理（热力学第一/二定律、热力学能、功、热、焓、熵、吉布斯自由能等），帮助学生深入理解能量的本质属性及转化过程的方向与限度规律。 3.化学动力学与能量转化速率：系统讲解化学反应速率、活化能等基本概念，分析浓度、温度等对化学反应速率的影响，引导学生掌握能量转化的速率规律与优化途径，同步介绍能源催化等学科前沿。 4.氧化还原反应与电化学能源：系统讲解氧化还原反应、化学势与电化学势、电极电势、能斯特方程、原电池等基础理论，结合锂离子电池、燃料电池等原理及构造的介绍，理解电化学能源存储与转换的核心机制，建立理论与技术的关联。 5.能源化学的现代应用：紧密衔接前述基础理论，串联讲解燃烧化学、二氧化碳化学转化、电化学能源技术（燃料电池、锂离子电池、电解水制氢）、光能源化学与光伏电池等现代应用，并通过 “能源应用课程报告” 的实践形式，引导学生主动调研、分析能源领域实际问题，强化理论应用能力。
Course Description
Fundamentals of Energy Chemistry is an introductory general chemistry course for the students of the Ziqiang College. The course provides students with a systematic grasp of core fundamental theories, including: Structure of Matter and Energy Materials, Chemical Thermodynamics, Kinetics and Energy Conversion, Redox Reactions and Electrochemistry. It also helps students understand the application of these theories in processes such as energy acquisition, storage, conversion and transmission. The main content of the course includes: Structure of Matter and Energy Materials: basic principles of material structure (atomic/molecular structure, chemical bonding, crystal structure, and states of matter) energy utilization and conversion. Chemical Thermodynamics and Energy Conversion: principles of chemical thermodynamics (First/Second Laws, internal energy, work, heat, enthalpy, entropy, Gibbs free energy, etc.) to understand the fundamental laws of energy conversion. Chemical Kinetics and Reaction Rates of Energy Conversion: basic concepts such as reaction rate and activation energy, the influence of concentration, temperature, etc., on reaction rate, to understand the speed and pathways of energy conversion; introduction of frontiers in energy catalysis. Redox Reactions and Electrochemical Energy: fundamental theories of redox reactions, chemical potential, electrochemical potential, electrode potential, Nernst equation, and galvanic cells, to understand the basic principles of electrochemical energy storage and conversion. Modern Applications of Chemistry and Energy: integrates modern energy technologies throughout the course, such as combustion chemistry, chemical conversion of CO2, fuel cells, lithium-ion batteries, water electrolysis for hydrogen production, and photochemical energy and photovoltaic cells, aiming to reinforce the students’ ability to use fundamental chemical principles to analyze energy-related problems.
