课程内容简介：
海洋是地球上覆盖面积最广阔的水体，是孕育生命的摇篮, 是气候系统的“调节器”。海洋学从19世纪70年代 起，从传统的自然地理学领域分化出来，形成了一门以物理海洋学、海洋化学、海洋地质学、海洋生物学四大 分支学科为主，各分支学科之间彼此交叉渗透的学科。本课程以海洋与气候变化为主线，在对海洋各门学科基 本知识介绍的基础上，引导学生对真实的海洋现象进行概念、过程和理论的探讨，并联系当今全球气候变暖， 帮助学生学习如何基于基本理论探寻真实世界复杂的海洋与气候现象。课程内容覆盖海洋的起源与历史、海水 的性质、海气相互作用、海水的运动、风生环流与热盐环流、全球变暖下海洋的响应、海洋对全球变暖的影响 及其相互作用、海洋生态系统和适应性、碳循环、地球系统模式等。
Course Description
The ocean is the most extensive body of water on Earth, the cradle of life, and the central regulator of the climate system. Since the 1870s, oceanography has separated from the traditional field of geography to form its own discipline encompassing four major sub-disciplines: physical oceanography, marine chemistry, marine geology, and marine biology; and the interplays among them. This course focuses on the ocean and climate change. Incorporating foundational knowledge from each of the four marine sub-disciplines, it guides students to explore the concepts, processes, and theories of ocean phenomena and the complexities of ocean and climate change in the context of global warming. The course covers the origins and evolution of the oceans; the nature of seawater; air-sea interactions; the movement of seawater, including wind-driven and thermohaline circulations; the oceans’ response to, impacts on, and interactions with global warming; marine ecosystems and adaptation; the carbon cycle; and ocean representations in Earth system models.
