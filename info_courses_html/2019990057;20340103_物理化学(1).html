课程内容简介：
物理化学是数学、物理和化学学科的交叉学科，是化学、化工、环境、生物工程、材料、药学等学科的理论基础课之一。物理化学借助物理学中的理论和实验方法研究化学变化的基本规律。当物质发生化学变化的时候，必然伴随着物理现象的发生。 物理化学就是从这些现象之间的密切联系入手，探究化学变化的基本规律，采用物理学的实验或者理论手段解决化学问题。 物理化学的主要内容包括化学热力学、化学动力学、电化学、胶体化学、表面化学、统计热力学等内容。上述内容将分成《物理化学1》（48学时）和《物理化学2》（48学时）两门课，分两个学期完成，总学时为96学时。 《物理化学1》的主要内容包括： 1. 物理化学的任务与研究方法 2. 气体与状态方程 3. 热力学第一定律及热化学 4. 热力学第二定律与热力学第三定律 5. 均相多组分系统 6. 多相系统相平衡 7. 化学反应平衡 通过上述内容学习，重点掌握化学热力学的基本概念、重要理论、研究方法和实际应用，初步建立分子观点以及微观与宏观之间的联系。上述内容将利用48个课内学时进行课堂讲授，学生需要利用96个课外学时完成复习相关概念的理解与习题练习以增强对于物理化学的理解。
Course Description
Physical Chemistry is an interdisciplinary field that combines mathematics, physics, and chemistry. It serves as one of the foundational theoretical courses for disciplines such as chemistry, chemical engineering, environmental science, bioengineering, materials science, and pharmaceuticals. Physical chemistry employs the theories and experimental methods of physics to study the fundamental laws governing chemical changes. When a substance undergoes a chemical change, it is inevitably accompanied by physical phenomena. Physical chemistry explores the fundamental laws of chemical changes by examining the close relationships between these phenomena, using experimental or theoretical approaches from physics to solve chemical problems. The main content of physical chemistry includes chemical thermodynamics, chemical kinetics, electrochemistry, colloid chemistry, surface chemistry, statistical thermodynamics, and more. These topics will be divided into two courses: Physical Chemistry 1 (48 credit hours) and Physical Chemistry 2 (48 credit hours), to be completed over two semesters, totaling 96 credit hours. Through the study of these topics, students will focus on mastering the basic concepts, key theories, research methods, and practical applications of chemical thermodynamics, while initially establishing a molecular perspective and the connection between microscopic and macroscopic phenomena. The above content will be delivered through 48 in-class credit hours of lectures, and students are expected to dedicate 96 out-of-class credit hours to reviewing related concepts and completing exercises to deepen their understanding of physical chemistry.
