课程内容简介：
本课程首先介绍基础医学研究内容、方法、与临床医学的关系，细胞组织的构成，机体的功能构成，机体的功能调节，机体的内稳态。组织学研究正常人体组织微观结构及其功能关系。组织学总论主要介绍组织学概述和四大组织类型，包括上皮组织、结缔组织、肌组织和神经组织。生理学从分子、细胞和组织器官水平揭示基本生命活动包括跨膜物质转运、细胞电活动、肌肉收缩等的基本机理，血液的组分和功能等内容，为医学院校的必修课程，也是各类执业医师考试的必考内容。胚胎学研究人体发生、器官形成，内容包括人体胚胎学总论(胚胎早期发生、胎膜胎盘等)、器官系统的发生及人类发育缺陷等内容。
Course Description
Histology is a fundamental course in medical education that provides students with a deep understanding of the microscopic structure of tissues and organs. The course covers the four major types of tissues: epithelial, connective, muscle, and neural tissues, and their functions in the human body. Medical students learn how to examine tissue samples under a microscope and identify different types of tissues, which is crucial in the diagnosis and treatment of diseases. Histology provides a comprehensive understanding of the microscopic structures and functions of tissues and organs. It is crucial for diagnosing diseases, surgical planning, and developing new treatments and therapies. Physiology is a fundamental course for medical students that provides a comprehensive understanding of how the human body works. It explains the function of important cells types, and the underlying molecular mechanisms. A thorough understanding of physiology is essential for medical students to comprehend the complex physiological processes that underlie normal human function and how these processes are altered in disease states，and to become competent doctors who can diagnose and treat patients effectively. Physiology is also necessary for medical students to understand the pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics of drugs used to treat various medical conditions. Embryology is a critical course for medical students as it provides a comprehensive understanding of human development from fertilization to birth. It covers gamete formation, fertilization, preimplantation development, implantation, placenta, fetal membrane, and embryo axis formation. A thorough knowledge of these processes is essential for medical students to understand the process underlying normal and abnormal development, which is critical for the diagnosis and management of congenital anomalies and genetic disorders.
